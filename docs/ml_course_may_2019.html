The Course: ML in Medical Bioinformatics

  • Offered by MedBioInfo (The Swedish Research School in Medical Bioinformatics)
  • Location: Linkping University, Sweden
  • Access the course material here
  • Easy to intermediate level
  • Covers the most essential/practical ML for applications
  • Excellent lecturers
    • Manuela Zucknick (Statistician)
    • Claudio Mirabello (NN expert)
  • Almost non-medical but I highly recommend it nonetheless!

Before the Course

Preparatory tasks (1 week)

  • Most tasks were Python-based
  • Use scikit-learn module for teaching ML

Reading

  • James et al.(2013). An Introduction to Statistical Learning with Applications in R. Springer
  • Neural Networks and Deep Learning, Michael Nielsen (online book)

During the course

Practical Exercises

  • Use jupyter-notebooks
  • Discussion: R vs Python for ML (beginners vs advanced)
  • Know your data, know your methods!

Applications in research and reproducibility (R)

  • Aure et al (2015), Integrated analysis reveals microRNA networks coordinately expressed with key proteins in breast cancer

Neural Networks and Deep Learning

  • Tensor Flow Playground
  • Keras: The Python Deep Learning library

Feature Importance

or why John participated in the course!

Can ML help us find biomarkers?

Methods

  • Variable selection
    • Correlation plots between the features and the response
    • Linear Regression Models with Regularization (LASSO, Elastic Nets)
  • Random Forests (feature selection is embedded in decision trees)
  • Use combination of methods!
  • Neural Networks can also work but need a lot of engineering
